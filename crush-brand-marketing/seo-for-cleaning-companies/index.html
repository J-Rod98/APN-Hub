The cleaning company problem

Recurring revenue makes cleaning SEO the best math in local marketing.

When a plumber wins a lead, it's one job. When a cleaning company wins a lead, it's often every two weeks for years. That's why ranking matters more in cleaning than almost any other trade — each position on Google compounds into recurring revenue.

Yet most cleaning websites are a single page with a phone number, competing against franchises with full sites and hundreds of reviews. You don't beat them with a bigger ad budget — you beat them with structure they're too slow to build.

Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions.

How do cleaning companies actually get found online?+

Two places: the Maps pack for "house cleaning near me" searches, and organic results for specific services like move-out or deep cleaning. Winning both takes an optimized Google Business Profile plus dedicated service pages — one without the other leaves leads on the table.

We do residential and commercial. Should the website separate them?+

Absolutely — they're different buyers with different questions. Homeowners want trust and easy booking; office managers want reliability, insurance, and invoicing. Separate pages speak to each and rank for each.

How important are reviews for a cleaning business?+

For a business people let into their homes, reviews are close to everything. Count, recency, and your responses all matter. A simple ask-after-every-clean process is one of the highest-return things a cleaning company can set up.
