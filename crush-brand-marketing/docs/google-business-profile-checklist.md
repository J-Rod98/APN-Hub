# Google Business Profile — Owner Checklist

Work through this top to bottom. Most items take minutes; consistency is what wins.
These steps happen at https://business.google.com — they can't be done from the website code.

## 1. Claim and verify

- [ ] Claim (or create) the profile for **Crush Brand Marketing** at business.google.com
- [ ] Verify by the method Google offers (video verification is common for service-area businesses)
- [ ] **Do NOT publish a home address.** Set the profile up as a **service-area business**: hide the address, list the areas served (Little Rock, North Little Rock, Sherwood, Maumelle, Conway, Cabot, Benton, Bryant)

## 2. Core settings

- [ ] Primary category: **Marketing agency** (alternatives: *Website designer* — pick the one matching the searches you most want; you can test and change later)
- [ ] Secondary categories: **Website designer**, **Internet marketing service**
- [ ] Business name: exactly **Crush Brand Marketing** — no keywords stuffed in ("Crush Brand Marketing | Web Design & SEO" violates guidelines and risks suspension)
- [ ] Phone: **(501) 500-4306** — the same number as the website, formatted the same way
- [ ] Website link: `https://www.crushbrandmarketing.com/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp`
- [ ] Appointment link: `https://www.crushbrandmarketing.com/website-audit/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp-appointment`
- [ ] Hours: set real hours you'll answer the phone

## 3. Services

Add each as a separate service with a 2–3 sentence description written like you talk:

- [ ] Website design ("Lead-generation websites for service businesses, from $697 landing pages to full custom builds…")
- [ ] Local SEO
- [ ] Google Business Profile optimization
- [ ] Website hosting & maintenance
- [ ] Website audit (free)

## 4. Photos

- [ ] Logo + cover photo (brand mark on the dark navy, consistent with the site)
- [ ] A real photo of Jonathan (people buy from people)
- [ ] Screenshots of real work (Tuff Bros site on a laptop/phone)
- [ ] Add 2–3 new photos monthly — recency is a ranking signal

## 5. Weekly rhythm (15 minutes)

- [ ] One post per week: a finished project, a plain-English tip, or an offer. Link with UTM: `?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp-post`
- [ ] Answer every question in Q&A (seed 3–5 yourself: "How much does a website cost?", "Do you work outside Little Rock?")
- [ ] Respond to **every** review within 48 hours — thank the good ones specifically, address any bad one calmly and factually

## 6. Reviews

- [ ] Get your review link: profile → "Ask for reviews" → copy the short URL
- [ ] Ask every happy client at the moment of delivery (when you hand over the finished site)
- [ ] Never buy, trade, or incentivize reviews; never review-gate (filtering who gets asked based on sentiment violates policy)

## 7. Tracking

- [ ] Check GBP performance monthly: calls, website clicks, direction requests
- [ ] The UTM parameters above make GBP traffic visible in Google Analytics as its own campaign
