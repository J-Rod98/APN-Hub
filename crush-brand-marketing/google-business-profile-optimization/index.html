Why the Maps pack matters

Local customers don't scroll. They call one of the top three.

When someone searches "mobile detailing near me" or "house cleaning Conway", Google shows a map with three businesses before anything else. Those three get the calls. Everyone below is fighting for leftovers.

Which three show up isn't random. Google weighs your profile's completeness, categories, reviews, photos, activity, and how consistently your business information appears across the web — plus the strength of the website behind it.

Most local profiles were claimed once, half-filled, and abandoned. That's the opening: a fully built, active profile routinely outranks bigger competitors who ignore theirs.

Signs your profile is underperforming

✕ You search your own service and competitors show up instead
✕ One generic category, no services listed
✕ A handful of photos from years ago
✕ Reviews trickling in once a quarter
✕ Service areas missing half the towns you actually work in
✕ No posts, no Q&A, no activity since you claimed it

Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions.

Isn't Google Business Profile free? Why pay for help?+

The profile is free — the rankings aren't. Categories, services, photo strategy, posting cadence, review velocity, and service-area settings all affect where you show up in the Maps pack, and most profiles have them wrong or empty. You're paying for the profile to actually produce calls.

Why am I not showing up in the Maps pack?+

Usually some combination of: wrong or missing categories, few recent reviews, thin service listings, inconsistent name/address/phone across the web, and a weak website behind the profile. An audit will show you exactly which ones apply to you.

Can you remove bad reviews?+

Nobody can remove legitimate negative reviews — be wary of anyone who claims otherwise. We can flag reviews that violate Google's policies, help you respond professionally, and build a steady flow of new 5-star reviews so one bad one stops defining your rating.

I'm a mobile business with no shopfront. Does this still work?+

Yes — Google supports service-area businesses like mobile detailers and lawn crews. Your address stays hidden and your profile shows the areas you serve. Setting this up correctly matters a lot, and it's frequently done wrong.
